Cassete Player

A cassette player, also known as a cassette deck or cassette recorder,
is an electronic device designed for playing and recording audio on compact
cassettes. It was a popular consumer audio format throughout the 1970s
and 1980s before being largely replaced by digital media.

Vinyl Records

Vinyl records, also known as phonograph records or simply "vinyl," are
analog audio storage mediums that were widely used for music
playback before the advent of digital formats. Vinyl records consist of
a flat, circular disc made of polyvinyl chloride (PVC) with a spiral groove
engraved on both sides.

Super Nintendo

The Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES), also known as
the Super Nintendo or Super Famicom (in Japan), is a home video
game console that was released by Nintendo in 1990. It is the
successor to the original Nintendo Entertainment System (NES)
and is one of the most iconic and beloved gaming consoles of its time.

Cassete Tape

A cassette tape, commonly referred to as a cassette, is a
magnetic tape recording format that was widely used for
audio storage and playback from the 1960s through the
1990s. It was a popular medium for music distribution,
personal recording, and portable audio.
